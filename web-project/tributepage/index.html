Oskar Schindler

A Hero of the Holocaust

The legacy of Oskar Schindler is proof that ethical action and personal bravery can prevail even in the most terrible situations. His persistent devotion to humanity and his courageous acts of disobedience against oppression represent a living reminder of the power of empathy and solidarity. Oskar Schindler's incredible bravery made a lasting impression on history and served as a constant reminder to all of us of how important it is to stand against injustice and defend the value and dignity of every human life.

Here's a time line of Oskar Schindler's life:

  • 1908 - Born in Zwittau, Austria-Hungary (now Svitavy, Czech Republic)
  • 1939 - Begins employing Jewish workers in his factory, initially for economic reasons, but later he uses his position to save his workers from deportation to concentration camps
  • 1944 - Establishes an additional factory in Brünnlitz (now Brněnec, Czech Republic) to protect his Jewish workers from the advancing Soviet Army
  • 1945 - After the war ends, Schindler is arrested as a Nazi collaborator but is released due to his efforts in saving Jewish lives
  • 1958 - Honored as Righteous Among the Nations by Yad Vashem in Israel for his heroic actions during the Holocaust
  • 1974 - Dies in Hildesheim, West Germany (now Germany)

"Schindler's life and actions serve as a beacon of hope and humanity during one of the darkest periods in human history, demonstrating the profound impact one individual can have on the lives of many."

-- Yad Vashem Holocaust Memorial Museum
